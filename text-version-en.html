Macdonald – The beginning

1864

The forgotten Father of Confederation

Let’s not forget Sir Étienne-Paschal Taché. This doctor turned politician came out of retirement to chair the Quebec conference in 1864 and promoted the 72 resolutions that would form the basis of Confederation: sadly, he died in 1865.

1867

John A. Macdonald

John A. Macdonald was a lawyer, businessman and politician. He was instrumental, along with Sir George-Étienne Cartier, in the negotiations that led to Confederation.

Cartier – Accomplishments

A key architect and Father of Confederation, Sir George-Étienne Cartier led Quebec into Confederation and later helped negotiate the entry of the Northwest Territories, Manitoba and British Columbia into Canada.
